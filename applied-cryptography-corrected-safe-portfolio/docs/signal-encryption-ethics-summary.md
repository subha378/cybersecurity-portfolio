# Encryption Ethics Summary — End-to-End Encryption and Backdoors

## Position
End-to-end encrypted messaging services should not be required to build government backdoors.

## Argument 1: Backdoors weaken security for everyone
A backdoor is still a vulnerability. Even if designed for lawful access, it can be discovered, misused, leaked, or exploited by malicious actors.

## Argument 2: Strong encryption protects civil liberties and the digital economy
End-to-end encryption protects ordinary users, journalists, lawyers, activists, businesses, healthcare systems, banking, and private communications.

## Argument 3: Backdoors are ineffective against serious criminals
Sophisticated criminals can move to foreign, open-source, or custom encryption tools. Weakening mainstream encryption may mostly harm ordinary users rather than the intended targets.

## Better Alternatives
- targeted device access with judicial oversight
- metadata analysis within legal limits
- human intelligence
- endpoint investigations
- lawful warrants focused on individuals, not mass weakening of encryption systems
